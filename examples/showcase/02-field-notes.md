# Field notes

Small observations from the plaintext era.

## On weight

A modern Electron app is ~150 MB. The text it renders is, on average, 12 KB. That's a four-order-of-magnitude tax on reading a thought someone had.

## On typography

Serif bodies read 8–12% faster than sans in long-form on high-DPI screens — every study since the iPad retina, every time. The rest of software forgot. peek remembers.

## On agents

The asymmetry is already here:

> *"Generate a 2-pager on our Q4 strategy."* — human, 8 seconds
> *"Read the 2-pager and summarize it."* — human, 4 minutes

The bottleneck moved. The reader is the expensive resource. peek exists to make that 4 minutes feel like 2.
